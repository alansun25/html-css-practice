HTML Entities

There are three reserved characters in HTML: < > and & You should always use HTML entities for these three characters.

Entities always begin with an ampersand and end with a semicolon. In between, you put a special code that your browser will interpret as a symbol. In this case, it interprets lt, gt, and amp as less-than, greater-than, and ampersand symbols, respectively. There’s a crap-ton of HTML entities. We’ll leave you to explore most of them on your own.

If you’re into “web typography,” you’ll also find yourself using curly quotes quite a bit (look at code for entities).
